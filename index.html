Mother Teresa

"Not all of us can do great things. But we can do small things with great love."

Mother Teresa, also known as Saint Teresa of Calcutta, was a Roman Catholic nun and missionary. She was born on August 26, 1910, and dedicated her life to serving the poor and sick in India.

Key Life Events

  • 1910: Born in Skopje
  • 1928: Joined the Sisters of Loreto
  • 1950: Founded the Missionaries of Charity
  • 1979: Received the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1997: Passed away
  • 2016: Canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta
